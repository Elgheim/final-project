The problem.

People often view Africa as being similar, though the differences in different regions are extreme. According to UNDP, Africa is often viewed as lacking educated workers, having low infrastructures and having weak judicial systems.

1. This scares investors and companies from investing in the continent.

2. Africa's reputation has been misrepresented for centuries, which would in turn hinder their growth globally.

African entrepreneurs face some of the world's toughest business conditions. The biggest challenge they face is risk-averse banks and lack of capital. Studies show that Sub-Saharan companies are 24% smaller than similar businesses across the globe.

The opportunity.

A report by the UN Conference on Trade and Development states that between 2006 and 2011, Africa had the highest rate of return on inflows of Foreign Direct Investment: 11,4%.

Africa was the most profitable continent for investors between 2006 and 2011. The continent has vast natural resources combined with a young and increasingly educated population. The continent as a whole, has one of the best possibilities for growth in the world.
