Our solution.

Problems.

•

Many are not investing in the African continent due to outdated beliefs, not knowing where to invest or because they are discouraged by language and cultural barriers. These factors scare away potential investors.

•

Charity and relief works is what most people think of when you say Africa. This creates a stereotype that all Africans are poor and need assistance. These stereotypes are so strongly held that they damage the potential growth of the continent.

•

Many African countries lack safe and secure banking, which are fundamental needs for businesses to survive and thrive. African entrepreneurs are also vulnerable to exploitation by foreign companies that take advantage and steal African innovations.

What can be done.

•

A large barrier for African entrepreneurs to get funding is the culture and language barrier for businesses and western investors. By tranining local staff to help these businesses, we can reduce these barriers.

•

Educate people on misconceptions about Africa. Africa was the most profitable continent from 2006 to 2011. By showing successful African businesses, we can overcome these misconceptions.

•

Safe and secure banking is a neccessity for businesses and entrepreneurs. By making local financial services better, we can enable businesses to succeed.
